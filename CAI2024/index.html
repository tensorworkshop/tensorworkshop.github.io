Overview


  Tensor Networks (TNs) are factorizations of high-order tensors into networks of low-order tensors, extensively explored in quantum physics, chemistry, and applied mathematics, which have seen a surge in application within machine learning and AI. They offer valuable capabilities for modeling multimodal data, compressing deep neural networks, and extending algorithms to high-dimensional data. TNs have effectively addressed challenges such as data completion, model compression, multimodal fusion, multitask learning, and learning theory. Their rapid integration into machine learning involves tasks like modeling probability functions and executing efficient TN computations on GPUs. Moreover, TNs have diverse applications across adversarial learning, explainable AI, large language models (LLMs), and machine learning in scientific domains. For example, in adversarial learning frameworks like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), tensors represent image data for generators and discriminators. Additionally, in explainable AI, tensors elucidate model weights and feature importance, aiding visualization and explanation of machine learning predictions. LLMs such as GPT leverage tensors to represent textual sequences for encoding and processing language information. In scientific domains such as physics, chemistry, and biology, tensor methods integrated with machine learning are utilized to analyze complex scientific data. The success of AlphaTensor further validates the efficacy of tensors across various fields.
  Quantum Tensor Networks in Machine Learning (QTNML) hold tremendous potential to propel advancements in AI technologies. Quantum machine learning offers quantum advantages, such as potentially exponential speedups in training and quadratic speedups in convergence, compared to classical machine learning. Tensor networks, on the other hand, offer robust simulations of quantum machine learning algorithms on classical computers. As a burgeoning interdisciplinary area, QTNML stands poised to amplify computational intelligence, transform machine learning innovations, and propel AI industrialization. Quantum algorithms, described through quantum circuits, form a subclass of tensor networks, establishing a clear interplay between classical tensor network contraction algorithms and executing tensor contractions on quantum processors. The nascent field of quantum-enhanced machine learning incorporates tools from tensor network theory to develop new quantum models and enhance the understanding of existing ones.
  The interplay among tensor networks, machine learning, and quantum algorithms is multifaceted. It extends beyond numerical methods, delving into the equivalence of tensor networks to various quantum circuits, evolving algorithms from the mathematics and physics communities, and connections to low-rank methods for learning. The convergence of tensor network algorithms with cutting-edge deep learning approaches is underway, fostering the emergence of a new community. This workshop aims to nurture discussions among researchers exploring innovative TN technology, spanning fundamental theory and algorithms for classical and quantum machine learning, as well as applications in computer vision, biomedical image processing, NLP, and more.
